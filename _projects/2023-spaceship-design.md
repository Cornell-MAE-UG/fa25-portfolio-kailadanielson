This semester in my class MAE 3230 Introductory Fluid Mechanics, we performed a fluid-mechanical dissection on a common, gas-powered wheedwhacker. To present our findings, my group made a video presentation. Here are my contributions to the project:

- Aided in the dissasembly and documentation of our weedwhacker. Primarily, I collaborated with a peer to take apart the two-stroke engine and flywheel system, although we did look at other components as well. Through the entire process, I took extensive notes and photos which I shared with my group and was later included in our presentation. 

- Helped write and edit our script. My largest contributions to the script included making the overall outline, and writing about the engine and carburetor components. I also provided suggestions and edits to the entire script. 

- Made detailed diagrams of the carburetor, showing the geometries and dimensions which we used later in our analysis. Additionally, helped create our visual presentation and slides. 

- Presented about the carburetor resovoir and diaphram, overview of the carburetor, and carburetor exit velocity. 

Overall, this was a very fun project to collaborate on, and helped solidify my fluid-mechanical knowledge through a fun, hands-on application. Please check out our final presentation below!
